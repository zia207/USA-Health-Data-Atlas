CONUS County-Level Cancer Incidence — Dataset Documentation

A tidy, long-format time series of annual county-level cancer incidence for the contiguous United States (48 states + DC), covering 2020–2025 across 15 cancer sites. The dataset is produced by cancer_incidence_pipeline.py, which ships real connectors to the authoritative sources and a reproducible synthetic generator that fills the target schema for every county × year × site.

Read this first — what “annual county incidence” is and is not

This is the single most important thing to understand before using the data.

Authoritative US county cancer incidence comes from State Cancer Profiles (NCI, built on CDC’s National Program of Cancer Registries and NCI’s SEER). That source does not publish a single-year county series. Three structural facts make a genuine annual 2020–2025 county panel impossible to obtain directly:

  1. County data is released as a 5-year pooled rate, not annually. Single-year county counts are too small and statistically unstable, so the published unit is the latest 5-year average (currently 2017–2021).
  2. Registry data lags roughly 2–4 years. As of mid-2026 the newest national figures reach about 2022; 2023–2025 county incidence has not been released.
  3. Small counts are suppressed. Counts under 16 are withheld, which censors most counties for most sites in any single year.

Because of this, the annual values in this dataset are a modeled construct, not observed registry counts. Every row is tagged so this is never ambiguous:

  • The source value ends in (SYNTHETIC_DEMO).
  • The data_status column records real-world availability per year (released, preliminary, or projected_not_released).

The pipeline’s live path (build_timeseries(use_synthetic=False)) returns the real 5-year-pooled county rates from State Cancer Profiles; the synthetic annual panel is calibrated to resemble those rates so the schema and analysis code can be developed against realistic structure.

data_status and suppression semantics

data_status encodes the real reporting timeline rather than pretending all six years are equally available:

Year(s) data_status Real-world meaning
2020–2022 released Published nationally (pooled/annual) by mid-2026
2023 preliminary Partial/early release; incomplete
2024–2025 projected_not_released Not yet available at county level

suppressed = True marks county × year × site cells with fewer than 16 cases, whose cases and age_adjusted_rate_per_100k are set to NaN. In this panel roughly 58% of cells are suppressed — this is the genuine texture of annual county incidence, concentrated in small-population counties and rarer sites, not a defect. Any national or state roll-up that sums only unsuppressed cells will therefore undercount; use the released pooled rates for accurate totals.

Synthetic generation (methodology)

When use_synthetic=True, each county × site is assigned a persistent log-normal geographic effect, and annual expected cases are drawn as

λ = (base_rate / 100,000) × sex_population × county_effect
      × site_year_drift(year) × covid_dip(year)
cases ~ Poisson(λ)

with these deliberate, documented features:

  • 2020 COVID dip: a ~10% reduction in 2020 with partial rebound in 2021, reflecting the well-documented pandemic disruption to screening and diagnosis.
  • Site-specific secular drift: small trends (e.g., lung falling, melanoma and thyroid rising) centered on 2022.
  • Registry suppression: counts of 1–15 are suppressed.
  • Reproducibility: fixed seed (207), so the panel regenerates identically.

The generator is calibrated for realistic shape, not truth. In a validation run, national All-Sites unsuppressed cases rose from ~1.32M (2020, dip) to ~1.47M (2022), and the site ranking came out Breast > Prostate > Lung > Colorectal > Melanoma — matching the real US ordering.

Usage

import pandas as pd

df = pd.read_parquet("conus_cancer_incidence_timeseries.parquet")

# 1. Work only with reported (unsuppressed) cells
reported = df[~df["suppressed"]]

# 2. One county's lung-cancer trend
tompkins = df.query("fips == '36109' and cancer_site == 'Lung & Bronchus'")

# 3. Wide pivot: age-adjusted rate by site across years for one county
wide = (df.query("fips == '36061'")               # New York County
          .pivot_table(index="year", columns="cancer_site",
                       values="age_adjusted_rate_per_100k"))

# 4. State-year totals for a site (unsuppressed only — see caveat above)
state_yr = (reported.query("cancer_site == 'Colon & Rectum'")
                    .groupby(["state_abbr", "year"])["cases"].sum())

# 5. Restrict to years that are realistically released
released = df[df["data_status"] == "released"]

To pull the real 5-year-pooled county rates instead of the synthetic panel:

from cancer_incidence_pipeline import PipelineConfig, build_timeseries

cfg = PipelineConfig(states=None, conus_only=True)   # or states=["NY"]
real = build_timeseries(cfg, use_synthetic=False)    # needs internet

Before trusting a live pull, verify the State Cancer Profiles cancer codes in SCP_CANCER_CODES against the site’s “Cancer:” dropdown — SCP’s internal numeric codes are idiosyncratic and are left flagged for confirmation.

Caveats and limitations

  • Modeled, not observed. Annual county values are synthetic. Use the live 5-year pooled rates for any real analysis or reporting.
  • Do not sum suppressed-out cells for totals. ~58% suppression makes unsuppressed sums systematic undercounts.
  • 2023–2025 are not real. Those years carry projected_not_released.
  • Age-adjusted vs crude. Compare counties using the age-adjusted rate; crude rate and pct_of_population do not account for age structure.
  • CONUS only. Alaska, Hawaii, and territories are excluded by design.
  • SCP code verification is required before a live fetch (see Usage).

Read this first — what this dataset is

This is the authoritative, observed county incidence data. Two things to keep in mind about its nature:

  1. It is a pooled snapshot, not an annual time series. State Cancer Profiles publishes county incidence as a single latest 5-year average (currently 2017–2021), because single-year county counts are too small and unstable to release. Every row represents the 2017–2021 pooled rate; there is no per-year dimension. (For a modeled annual view, see the separate 2020–2025 synthetic panel; for observed annual counts, CDC WONDER / USCS is the source, heavily suppressed at county level.)
  2. Suppressed cells are absent, not zero. County × site combinations with fewer than 16 cases are not published and therefore do not appear as rows. Rarer sites cover far fewer counties than common ones (Cervix ~736 vs All Cancer Sites ~2,994). Never impute zero for a missing combination.

Everything in the file is real: the source is StateCancerProfiles, the data_status is released_5yr_pooled, and rates are computed by NCI/CDC in SEER*Stat.

Coverage semantics

Three real coverage facts — features of the source, not defects:

  • Kansas is entirely absent. Kansas law restricts release of county-level cancer incidence, so no Kansas counties appear in State Cancer Profiles incidence at all. (Note the contrast with the mortality dataset, which does include Kansas, because death-certificate data via NVSS is not restricted.)
  • DC is state-level only in State Cancer Profiles incidence and is therefore absent from the county table. Together with Kansas, this yields 47 states.
  • Suppression is structural. County × site cells with fewer than 16 cases are not published and are absent as rows — rarer sites cover fewer counties. Absence is not zero.

Validation

The file matches known published values:

  • National all-sites age-adjusted rate ≈ 448.6 with ~1.82M average annual cases, matching US Cancer Statistics.
  • Summing county average_annual_count for All Cancer Sites gives ~1.79M cases/yr against the real ~1.8M (the small gap is suppressed/absent small counties).
  • Spot checks — Tompkins Co., NY all-sites 462.7; Los Angeles Co. 381.3 (42,579 cases/yr); New York Co. lung 40.6, falling; Harris Co., TX prostate 112.8 — all match State Cancer Profiles.

Read this first — what the annual series is, and how it is derived

This is the single most important thing to understand before using the data.

State Cancer Profiles publishes county cancer mortality as a single “Latest 5-year average” (currently 2019–2023) age-adjusted rate, together with a published recent annual percent change (APC) estimated by joinpoint regression. There is no observed single-year county mortality series in the bulk data; that exists only in CDC WONDER (Underlying Cause of Death, ICD-10 C00–C97, annual, county, counts <10 suppressed).

The annual series here is therefore a transparent reconstruction from two real published quantities per county × site:

rate(year)   = pooled_rate         × (1 + APC/100) ** (year − 2021)
deaths(year) = average_annual_count × (1 + APC/100) ** (year − 2021)

where 2021 is the midpoint of the 2019–2023 window. Both the level (pooled_rate_2019_2023) and the trend (recent_5yr_trend_pct) are real and retained as columns, so the reconstruction is fully auditable. The series geometrically centers on the published pooled rate at 2021.

Consequences to keep in mind:

  • These are reconstructed annual values, not observed death counts. The strength of the reconstruction is that its level and trend are both real; its limitation is that it assumes a smooth constant-APC path within the window and cannot capture year-specific shocks (e.g., a single anomalous year).
  • 82% of county × site cells carry a real published APC and are trended (data_status = reconstructed_trended). The remaining ~18% have no published trend and are held flat at the pooled rate (data_status = reconstructed_flat).
  • For genuinely observed annual county mortality, use CDC WONDER. The builder includes a documented cdc_wonder_county_cancer_mortality() helper describing that request.
