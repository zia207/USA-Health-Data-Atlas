USA-Health Data Atlas is a unified portal for public-health and place-based research at the county and state level. Instead of juggling separate portals, spreadsheets, and APIs, you can browse authoritative national datasets in one consistent interface — with choropleth maps, KPI summaries, SQL queries, and optional overlays (Superfund sites, air quality, soil geochemistry).

Each built-in explorer loads curated county-level data from sources such as County Health Rankings, CDC PLACES, CDC/ATSDR SVI, NCI State Cancer Profiles, NCHS overdose mortality, NIMHD HDPulse mortality, U.S. Census PEP demography, the Religion Census (RCMS/USRC), and MIT Election Lab presidential returns. The EDA & Model Development workbench joins these layers on fips so you can build modeling dataframes, run descriptive statistics, spatial analyses, and prototype machine-learning models in the browser.

  • Map — state, county, or tract views with adjustable palettes and basemaps
  • Query — AlaSQL tables for filtering, ranking, and aggregation
  • Model — pipeline from outcome Y and feature X selection to exportable CSV
  • Document — in-page dataset guides linked from each explorer

Social Vulnerability Index (SVI)

CDC/ATSDR’s SVI ranks communities on social factors that affect resilience to disasters and health stressors — socioeconomic status, household composition, minority status/language, and housing/transportation. Explore overall SVI and all indicators for states, counties, and census tracts (2022).

Built-in explorer · NCI / CDC

USA Cancer Atlas

County-level cancer incidence and mortality for the contiguous U.S. from State Cancer Profiles — modeled annual incidence (2020–2025), observed 5-year pooled incidence (2017–2021), and APC-reconstructed mortality (2019–2023). Map by cancer site and year, summarize with SQL, and read full dataset documentation in-page.

Open Cancer Atlas State Cancer Profiles Documentation
Built-in explorer · CDC / NCHS

USA Overdose Mortality

County-level drug-overdose and opioid-related mortality for the contiguous U.S. — three live CDC/NCHS source layers: model-based county estimates (2003–2021), VSRR provisional county counts (2020→), and VSRR state opioid totals allocated to counties (2020→). Map by year, compare sources, summarize with SQL, and read full dataset documentation in-page.
